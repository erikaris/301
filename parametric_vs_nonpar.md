# Parametric vs Non-parametric / Ordinal / Paired Tests

* Check this [decision tree](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/erikaris/301/7a22e2dba773be57a89ca85d2ee1f3f8bd3b0707/stats_key.svg).

| **No.** | **Purpose / Data Type** | **Parametric Test (Normal / Interval)** | **Non-parametric / Ordinal / Robust Alternative** | **Notes** | **R script example** | **Scenario & Research Question** | **H0 / H1** |
| ------ | ------------------------ | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | --------- | --------------------- | --------------------------------- | ----------- |
| 1 | **One-sample location (interval/ratio)** | One-sample t-test | Wilcoxon signed-rank; Sign test | Wilcoxon assumes symmetric distribution; Sign test works for skewed data. | `t.test(x, mu=0)`<br>`wilcox.test(x, mu=0)` | *Scenario:* A company wants to check if mean daily login time differs from a known benchmark.<br>*RQ:* “Is the mean login time significantly different from 5 minutes?” | **H0:** Mean = 5 min<br>**H1:** Mean ≠ 5 min |
| 2 | **Two independent samples (interval/ratio, equal variances)** | Independent t-test (student t) | Mann–Whitney U; 2-sample KS | Student t-test assumes equal variances; MW tests ranks. | `t.test(x, y, var.equal=TRUE)`<br>`wilcox.test(x, y)` | *Scenario:* Compare task completion times between new UI vs old UI.<br>*RQ:* “Does the new interface reduce task completion time?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2<br>**H1:** μ1 ≠ μ2 |
| 3 | **Two independent samples (interval/ratio, unequal variances)** | Welch’s t-test | Mann–Whitney U; KS | Welch for unequal variances. | `t.test(x, y)`<br>`wilcox.test(x, y)` | *Scenario:* Compare exam scores between two teaching methods with unequal group variability.<br>*RQ:* “Do the methods produce different mean scores?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2<br>**H1:** μ1 ≠ μ2 |
| 4 | **Two paired samples (interval/ratio)** | Paired t-test | Wilcoxon signed-rank; Sign test | Applied to paired differences. | `t.test(x, y, paired=TRUE)`<br>`wilcox.test(x, y, paired=TRUE)` | *Scenario:* Measure users’ stress level before/after meditation training.<br>*RQ:* “Does training lower stress?” | **H0:** Mean difference = 0<br>**H1:** Mean difference ≠ 0 |
| 5 | **≥3 independent groups (interval/ratio, equal variances)** | Welch ANOVA → **Tukey** | Kruskal–Wallis → Dunn | KW tests medians/ranks. | `oneway.test(y ~ g, var.equal=TRUE)`<br>`TukeyHSD(aov(y~g))`<br>`kruskal.test(y~g)` | *Scenario:* Compare average satisfaction across 3 product versions.<br>*RQ:* “Do mean satisfaction ratings differ among versions?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2 = μ3<br>**H1:** At least one μ differs |
| 6 | **≥3 independent groups (interval/ratio, unequal variances)** | One-way ANOVA → **Games-Howell** | Kruskal–Wallis; permutation ANOVA | KW tests medians/ranks. | `oneway.test(y ~ g)`<br>`oneway(..., posthoc="games-howell")` | *Scenario:* Compare battery life across 3 device models with unequal variances.<br>*RQ:* “Are there differences in mean battery life?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2 = μ3<br>**H1:** At least one μ differs |
| 7 | **≥3 related/repeated measures** | Repeated-measures ANOVA | Friedman test | Friedman = rank version. | `aov(y ~ cond + Error(id/cond))`<br>`friedman.test(...)` | *Scenario:* Measure performance of same users under 3 interface modes.<br>*RQ:* “Does performance differ across modes?” | **H0:** All repeated measures means are equal<br>**H1:** At least one differs |
| 8 | **Two-way factorial (interval/ratio)** | Two-way ANOVA | Aligned Rank Transform (ART) ANOVA; Scheirer–Ray–Hare (SRH) test; permutation factorial ANOVA | ART handles interactions. | `aov(y ~ A*B)`<br>`art(y ~ A*B)` | *Scenario:* Study effect of interface type × device type on accuracy.<br>*RQ:* “Are there main effects or interactions?” | **H0:** No main or interaction effects<br>**H1:** At least one main or interaction effect exists |
| 9 | **ANCOVA (factor(s) + covariate)** | ANCOVA | Quade; permutation ANCOVA; robust regression | Quade ranks adjusted values. | `anova(lm(y ~ g + cov))`<br>`quade.test(...)` | *Scenario:* Compare productivity across teams while adjusting for experience.<br>*RQ:* “Do teams differ after controlling for experience?” | **H0:** Factor has no effect controlling for covariate<br>**H1:** Factor has effect controlling for covariate |
| 10 | **Correlation (interval/ratio)** | Pearson | Spearman; Kendall | Spearman/Kendall for monotonic relationships. | `cor.test(..., "pearson")`<br>`cor.test(..., "spearman")` | *Scenario:* Relationship between number of features used and retention rate.<br>*RQ:* “Is feature usage correlated with retention?” | **H0:** ρ = 0<br>**H1:** ρ ≠ 0 |
| 11 | **Regression coefficients (interval/ratio)** | t-test for slope (OLS) | Robust regression; Theil-Sen; permutation tests | Robust reduces outlier influence. | `lm(y ~ x)`<br>`rlm(y ~ x)` | *Scenario:* Predict satisfaction from response time.<br>*RQ:* “Does response time significantly predict satisfaction?” | **H0:** β = 0<br>**H1:** β ≠ 0 |
| 12 | **Goodness-of-fit (categorical)** | Chi-square GOF / independence | Fisher; permutation | Use Fisher for small cells. | `chisq.test(tbl)`<br>`fisher.test(tbl)` | *Scenario:* Compare observed vs expected distribution of app categories used.<br>*RQ:* “Does usage differ from expected proportions?” | **H0:** Observed = expected frequencies<br>**H1:** Observed ≠ expected |
| 13 | **Paired binary data** | — | McNemar test | Tests change in paired dichotomous outcomes. | `mcnemar.test(tbl)` | *Scenario:* Test whether users answered correctly before vs after training.<br>*RQ:* “Did accuracy change after training?” | **H0:** Proportions before = after<br>**H1:** Proportions before ≠ after |
| 14 | **Ordinal data (independent samples)** | — | Mann–Whitney U / Kruskal–Wallis | Ranks preserve order. | `wilcox.test(x, y)`<br>`kruskal.test(y~g)` | *Scenario:* Compare Likert satisfaction ratings between two groups.<br>*RQ:* “Do groups differ in satisfaction?” | **H0:** Distributions are equal<br>**H1:** Distributions differ |
| 15 | **Ordinal data (paired samples)** | — | Wilcoxon signed-rank | Tests median shift. | `wilcox.test(x, y, paired=TRUE)` | *Scenario:* Users rate usability on a 1–5 scale before/after redesign.<br>*RQ:* “Did usability ratings change?” | **H0:** Median difference = 0<br>**H1:** Median difference ≠ 0 |
| 16 | **Normality / distribution check** | Shapiro–Wilk, KS | — | If rejected → use non-parametric alternatives. | `shapiro.test(x)`<br>`ks.test(...)` | *Scenario:* Check whether response times follow a normal distribution.<br>*RQ:* “Is the distribution normal?” | **H0:** Data follow normal distribution<br>**H1:** Data do not follow normal distribution |
| 17 | **Two independent proportions (binary data)** | Two-proportion z-test | Fisher; Chi-square | Large-sample approx; Fisher for small counts. | `prop.test(c(x1,x2), c(n1,n2))` | *Scenario:* Compare conversion rates between version A and B.<br>*RQ:* “Is the conversion rate different between versions?” | **H0:** p1 = p2<br>**H1:** p1 ≠ p2 |
| 18 | **Logistic regression (binary outcome)** | Logistic regression | — | Models probability of binary outcome. | `glm(y ~ x, family=binomial)` | *Scenario:* Predict likelihood of user churn based on login frequency.<br>*RQ:* “Does login frequency affect churn probability?” | **H0:** β = 0 (predictor has no effect)<br>**H1:** β ≠ 0 (predictor affects outcome) |
| 19 | **Kaplan-Meier (survival analysis)** | — | Kaplan-Meier estimator | Non-parametric estimate of survival function. | `survival::survfit(Surv(time, status) ~ group)` | *Scenario:* Compare time to feature adoption between two user groups.<br>*RQ:* “Do groups differ in time to adoption?” | **H0:** Survival functions equal between groups<br>**H1:** Survival functions differ |
| 20 | **Cox proportional hazards regression** | Cox regression | — | Models hazard ratio controlling covariates. | `survival::coxph(Surv(time,status) ~ x1 + x2)` | *Scenario:* Assess effect of login frequency and age on time to churn.<br>*RQ:* “Do predictors significantly affect hazard of churn?” | **H0:** Hazard ratio = 1 (no effect)<br>**H1:** Hazard ratio ≠ 1 (effect exists) |
