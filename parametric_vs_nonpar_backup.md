# Parametric vs Non-parametric / Ordinal / Paired Tests

* Check this [decision tree](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/erikaris/301/7a22e2dba773be57a89ca85d2ee1f3f8bd3b0707/stats_key.svg).

* Ask yourself: what is the dependent variables?
  - If outcome is:
    - Continuous (e.g., blood pressure, enzyme level, cholesterol) → Parametric tests possible
    - Categorical (e.g., yes/no disease) → Chi-square or logistic regression
    - Ordinal (e.g., pain score 1–5) → Possibly non-parametric

| 2 | **Two independent samples (interval/ratio, equal variances)** | Independent t-test (student t) | Mann–Whitney U; 2-sample KS | Student t-test assumes equal variances; MW tests ranks. | `t.test(x, y, var.equal=TRUE)`<br>`wilcox.test(x, y)` | *Scenario:* Compare task completion times between new UI vs old UI.<br>*RQ:* “Does the new interface reduce task completion time?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2<br>**H1:** μ1 ≠ μ2 | Report group means, SDs, t, df, p. | 3 | **Two independent samples (interval/ratio, unequal variances)** | Welch’s t-test | Mann–Whitney U; KS | Welch for unequal variances. | `t.test(x, y)`<br>`wilcox.test(x, y)` | *Scenario:* Compare exam scores between two teaching methods with unequal group variability.<br>*RQ:* “Do the methods produce different mean scores?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2<br>**H1:** μ1 ≠ μ2 | Report means, SDs, Welch t, df, p. | 4 | **Two paired samples (interval/ratio)** | Paired t-test | Wilcoxon signed-rank; Sign test | Applied to paired differences. | `t.test(x, y, paired=TRUE)`<br>`wilcox.test(x, y, paired=TRUE)` | *Scenario:* Measure users’ stress level before/after meditation training.<br>*RQ:* “Does training lower stress?” | **H0:** Mean difference = 0<br>**H1:** Mean difference ≠ 0 | Report mean difference, SD, t, df, p. Example: *Stress decreased significantly, t(29)=2.95, p=.006.* |
| 5 | **≥3 independent groups (interval/ratio, equal variances)** | Welch ANOVA → **Tukey** | Kruskal–Wallis → Dunn | KW tests medians/ranks. | `oneway.test(y ~ g, var.equal=TRUE)`<br>`TukeyHSD(aov(y~g))`<br>`kruskal.test(y~g)` | *Scenario:* Compare average satisfaction across 3 product versions.<br>*RQ:* “Do mean satisfaction ratings differ among versions?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2 = μ3<br>**H1:** At least one μ differs | Report F, df, p + post hoc. | 6 | **≥3 independent groups (interval/ratio, unequal variances)** | One-way ANOVA → **Games-Howell** | Kruskal–Wallis; permutation ANOVA | KW tests medians/ranks. | `oneway.test(y ~ g)`<br>`oneway(..., posthoc="games-howell")` | *Scenario:* Compare battery life across 3 device models with unequal variances.<br>*RQ:* “Are there differences in mean battery life?” | **H0:** μ1 = μ2 = μ3<br>**H1:** At least one μ differs | Report Welch F, df, p + Games-Howell. | 7 | **≥3 related/repeated measures** | Repeated-measures ANOVA | Friedman test | Friedman = rank version. | `aov(y ~ cond + Error(id/cond))`<br>`friedman.test(...)` | *Scenario:* Measure performance of same users under 3 interface modes.<br>*RQ:* “Does performance differ across modes?” | **H0:** All repeated measures means are equal<br>**H1:** At least one differs | Report F, df, p (or χ² for Friedman). | 9 | **ANCOVA (factor(s) + covariate)** | ANCOVA | Quade; permutation ANCOVA | Quade ranks adjusted values. | `anova(lm(y ~ g + cov))`<br>`quade.test(...)` | *Scenario:* Compare productivity across teams while adjusting for experience.<br>*RQ:* “Do teams differ after controlling for experience?” | **H0:** Factor has no effect controlling for covariate<br>**H1:** Factor has effect controlling for covariate | Report adjusted means, F, df, p. Example: *Group effect remained significant, F(2,45)=3.98, p=.026.* |
| 10 | **Correlation (interval/ratio)** | Pearson | Spearman; Kendall | Spearman/Kendall for monotonic relationships. | `cor.test(..., "pearson")`<br>`cor.test(..., "spearman")` | *Scenario:* Relationship between number of features used and retention rate.<br>*RQ:* “Is feature usage correlated with retention?” | **H0:** ρ = 0<br>**H1:** ρ ≠ 0 | Report r, p, n. Example: *r=.45, p=.002.* |
| 11 | **Regression coefficients (interval/ratio)** | t-test for slope (OLS) | Robust regression | Robust reduces outlier influence. | `lm(y ~ x)`<br>`rlm(y ~ x)` | *Scenario:* Predict satisfaction from response time.<br>*RQ:* “Does response time significantly predict satisfaction?” | **H0:** β = 0<br>**H1:** β ≠ 0 | Report β, SE, t, p. Example: *β=-0.32, p=.004.* |
| 12 | **Goodness-of-fit (categorical)** | Chi-square GOF | Fisher; permutation | Use Fisher for small cells. | `chisq.test(tbl)`<br>`fisher.test(tbl)` | *Scenario:* Compare observed vs expected distribution of app categories used.<br>*RQ:* “Does usage differ from expected proportions?” | **H0:** Observed = expected frequencies<br>**H1:** Observed ≠ expected | Report χ², df, p. | 13 | **Paired binary data** | — | McNemar test | Tests change in paired dichotomous outcomes. | `mcnemar.test(tbl)` | *Scenario:* Test whether users answered correctly before vs after training.<br>*RQ:* “Did accuracy change after training?” | **H0:** Proportions before = after<br>**H1:** Proportions before ≠ after | Report χ², p. | 14 | **Ordinal data (independent samples)** | — | Mann–Whitney / Kruskal–Wallis | Ranks preserve order. | `wilcox.test(x, y)`<br>`kruskal.test(y~g)` | *Scenario:* Compare Likert satisfaction ratings between two groups.<br>*RQ:* “Do groups differ in satisfaction?” | **H0:** Distributions are equal<br>**H1:** Distributions differ | Report U or H, p. | 15 | **Ordinal data (paired samples)** | — | Wilcoxon signed-rank | Tests median shift. | `wilcox.test(x, y, paired=TRUE)` | *Scenario:* Users rate usability on a 1–5 scale before/after redesign.<br>*RQ:* “Did usability ratings change?” | **H0:** Median difference = 0<br>**H1:** Median difference ≠ 0 | Report V (or Z), p. Example: *V=120, p=.021.* |
| 16 | **Normality / distribution check** | Shapiro–Wilk, KS | — | If rejected → use non-parametric alternatives. | `shapiro.test(x)`<br>`ks.test(...)` | *Scenario:* Check whether response times follow a normal distribution.<br>*RQ:* “Is the distribution normal?” | **H0:** Data follow normal distribution<br>**H1:** Data do not follow normal distribution | Report W, p. | 17 | **Two independent proportions (binary data)** | Two-proportion z-test | Fisher; Chi-square | Large-sample approx; Fisher for small counts. | `prop.test(c(x1,x2), c(n1,n2))` | *Scenario:* Compare conversion rates between version A and B.<br>*RQ:* “Is the conversion rate different between versions?” | **H0:** p1 = p2<br>**H1:** p1 ≠ p2 | Report proportions, χ² or z, p. | 18 | **Logistic regression (binary outcome)** | Logistic regression | — | Models probability of binary outcome. | `glm(y ~ x, family=binomial)` | *Scenario:* Predict likelihood of user churn based on login frequency.<br>*RQ:* “Does login frequency affect churn probability?” | **H0:** β = 0 (predictor has no effect)<br>**H1:** β ≠ 0 (predictor affects outcome) | Report OR, CI, p. Example: *OR=0.75, 95% CI [0.60,0.90], p=.003.* |
| 19 | **Kaplan-Meier (survival analysis)** | — | Kaplan-Meier estimator | Non-parametric estimate of survival function. | `survival::survfit(Surv(time, status) ~ group)` | *Scenario:* Compare time to feature adoption between two user groups.<br>*RQ:* “Do groups differ in time to adoption?” | **H0:** Survival functions equal between groups<br>**H1:** Survival functions differ | Report median survival + log-rank test. | 20 | **Cox proportional hazards regression** | Cox regression | — | Models hazard ratio controlling covariates. | `survival::coxph(Surv(time,status) ~ x1 + x2)` | *Scenario:* Assess effect of login frequency and age on time to churn.<br>*RQ:* “Do predictors significantly affect hazard of churn?” | **H0:** Hazard ratio = 1 (no effect)<br>**H1:** Hazard ratio ≠ 1 (effect exists) | Report HR, CI, p. Example: *HR=1.45, p=.008.* |
