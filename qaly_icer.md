# Economic Evaluation: QALY and ICER

## What are QALYs and ICERs?

In health economics, decision-makers often need to determine whether a new treatment provides sufficient health benefits to justify its additional cost.

Two key measures are used:

### QALY (Quality-Adjusted Life Year)

QALY combines:

1. Length of life (survival)
2. Quality of life (health utility)

The basic idea is that:

* 1 year in perfect health = 1 QALY
* 1 year at 50% health quality = 0.5 QALY
* Death = 0 QALY

Formula:

QALY = Utility \times Time

where:

* Utility = health-related quality of life score (typically between 0 and 1)
* Time = years lived

---

### ICER (Incremental Cost-Effectiveness Ratio)

ICER compares two interventions:

* Existing treatment (Comparator)
* New treatment (Intervention)

Formula:

ICER = \frac{Cost_{Intervention}-Cost_{Comparator}}{QALY_{Intervention}-QALY_{Comparator}}

ICER tells us:

> How much extra money must be spent to gain one additional QALY.

---

# Full Case Study

## Scenario

A public health department is evaluating a new diabetes management programme.

Current practice:

* Standard care

New intervention:

* Intensive diabetes management programme

The programme aims to:

* Improve quality of life
* Reduce complications
* Extend life expectancy

Researchers follow patients for 5 years.

---

# Step 1: Collect Data

Assume:

## Standard Care Group

| Patient | Utility | Years Lived |
| ------- | ------- | ----------- |
| 1       | 0.65    | 5           |
| 2       | 0.70    | 5           |
| 3       | 0.60    | 5           |
| 4       | 0.75    | 5           |
| 5       | 0.68    | 5           |

Average annual healthcare cost:

£12,000 per patient

---

## Intensive Programme Group

| Patient | Utility | Years Lived |
| ------- | ------- | ----------- |
| 1       | 0.80    | 5           |
| 2       | 0.82    | 5           |
| 3       | 0.78    | 5           |
| 4       | 0.85    | 5           |
| 5       | 0.79    | 5           |

Average annual healthcare cost:

£15,000 per patient

---

# Step 2: Calculate QALYs

## Standard Care

Patient 1:

0.65 × 5

= 3.25 QALYs

Patient 2:

0.70 × 5

= 3.50 QALYs

Patient 3:

0.60 × 5

= 3.00 QALYs

Patient 4:

0.75 × 5

= 3.75 QALYs

Patient 5:

0.68 × 5

= 3.40 QALYs

---

Average QALY:

(3.25 + 3.50 + 3.00 + 3.75 + 3.40)

÷ 5

= 3.38 QALYs

---

## Intensive Programme

Patient 1:

0.80 × 5

= 4.00 QALYs

Patient 2:

0.82 × 5

= 4.10 QALYs

Patient 3:

0.78 × 5

= 3.90 QALYs

Patient 4:

0.85 × 5

= 4.25 QALYs

Patient 5:

0.79 × 5

= 3.95 QALYs

---

Average QALY:

(4.00 + 4.10 + 3.90 + 4.25 + 3.95)

÷ 5

= 4.04 QALYs

---

# Step 3: Calculate Incremental Cost

Average cost:

Standard care:

£12,000

Intensive programme:

£15,000

Incremental cost:

£15,000 − £12,000

= £3,000

---

# Step 4: Calculate Incremental QALY

Average QALY:

Intervention:

4.04

Comparator:

3.38

Incremental QALY:

4.04 − 3.38

= 0.66

---

# Step 5: Calculate ICER

ICER:

£3,000 ÷ 0.66

= £4,545.45 per QALY

---

# Final Results Table

| Measure          | Standard Care | Intensive Programme |
| ---------------- | ------------- | ------------------- |
| Average Cost     | £12,000       | £15,000             |
| Average QALY     | 3.38          | 4.04                |
| Incremental Cost | -             | £3,000              |
| Incremental QALY | -             | 0.66                |
| ICER             | -             | £4,545.45/QALY      |

---

# Interpretation

The intensive programme costs an additional £3,000 per patient.

However, patients gain:

0.66 additional QALYs.

The ICER is:

£4,545.45 per QALY gained.

This means:

> Every additional QALY generated by the programme costs approximately £4,545.

Since this value is well below commonly cited UK willingness-to-pay thresholds (often around £20,000-£30,000 per QALY), the intervention would generally be considered cost-effective.

# How to Do This in R

## Create Data

```r
# Create utility scores for standard care patients
utility_standard <- c(0.65, 0.70, 0.60, 0.75, 0.68)

# Create utility scores for intervention patients
utility_intervention <- c(0.80, 0.82, 0.78, 0.85, 0.79)

# Assume all patients survive 5 years
years <- 5

# Cost per patient
cost_standard <- 12000
cost_intervention <- 15000
```

---

## Calculate Individual QALYs

```r
# Calculate QALYs for standard care
qaly_standard <- utility_standard * years

# Calculate QALYs for intervention
qaly_intervention <- utility_intervention * years

# Display results
qaly_standard
qaly_intervention
```

Output:

```r
[1] 3.25 3.50 3.00 3.75 3.40

[1] 4.00 4.10 3.90 4.25 3.95
```

---

## Calculate Mean QALYs

```r
# Calculate average QALY for standard care
mean_qaly_standard <- mean(qaly_standard)

# Calculate average QALY for intervention
mean_qaly_intervention <- mean(qaly_intervention)

# Display averages
mean_qaly_standard
mean_qaly_intervention
```

Output:

```r
[1] 3.38

[1] 4.04
```

---

## Calculate Incremental Values

```r
# Additional cost of intervention
incremental_cost <- cost_intervention - cost_standard

# Additional QALYs generated
incremental_qaly <- mean_qaly_intervention -
                    mean_qaly_standard

# Display results
incremental_cost
incremental_qaly
```

Output:

```r
[1] 3000

[1] 0.66
```

---

## Calculate ICER

```r
# Calculate ICER
icer <- incremental_cost / incremental_qaly

# Display ICER
icer
```

Output:

```r
[1] 4545.455
```

---

## Create Summary Table

```r
# Create summary table
results <- data.frame(
  Measure = c(
    "Average Cost",
    "Average QALY"
  ),
  Standard_Care = c(
    cost_standard,
    mean_qaly_standard
  ),
  Intervention = c(
    cost_intervention,
    mean_qaly_intervention
  )
)

# Print table
results
```

Output:

```r
         Measure Standard_Care Intervention
1   Average Cost      12000.00     15000.00
2   Average QALY          3.38         4.04
```

Transform → Compute Variable


Calculate:

* Mean Cost by Group

Manually calculate:

```text

```

ICER:

```text
3000 / 0.66 = 4545.45
```

# Common Student Mistakes

### Mistake 1

Confusing utility with QALY.

Wrong:

"Utility is 0.8 therefore QALY is 0.8."

Correct:

QALY = utility × time.

0.8 × 5 = 4.0 QALYs.

---

### Mistake 2

Using total cost rather than incremental cost.

Wrong:

```text
15000 / 4.04
```

Correct:

```text
(15000 - 12000)
/
(4.04 - 3.38)
```

---

### Mistake 3

Not interpreting ICER.

A number alone is not enough.

Always explain:

"The intervention costs £X per additional QALY gained."
